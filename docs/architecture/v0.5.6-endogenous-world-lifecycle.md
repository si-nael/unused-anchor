# v0.5.6 Endogenous World Lifecycle

Date: 2026-07-17

Status: completed bounded lifecycle-and-language release

## Closed claim

`v0.5.6` closes one finite causal-world seam: an active Bubble's own irreversible internal law may actualize a declared latent world, and an active world may retire itself, without the host or World Will selecting either lifecycle transition. Birth, later child action, retirement, residue, lineage, persistence, inspection, source lowering, and replay remain one typed evidence contract.

This is causal lifecycle, not a renamed observation collapse, a branch that did not occur, deletion, or dynamic schema generation.

## Lifecycle state and bound

World existence follows the acyclic rank

```text
latent < active < retired
```

This rank records irreversible provenance and does not define metric duration or a universal clock. Initially active worlds can retire but cannot regress to latent. Initially latent worlds can be born once and later retire. For `A` initially active and `L` initially latent declared worlds, one continuation has at most

```text
A + 2L
```

lifecycle transitions.

## Birth

- Only an irreversible internal law can carry `spawn-world(targetWorldId)`.
- The target must be a declared latent world and cannot be the source world.
- Several commuting laws that jointly cause one birth produce one spawn event containing every direct law cause.
- The event records parent worlds, lineage, exact cause ids, and `hostSelection: false`.
- The child's fields and later laws cite the spawn event; its laws, seas, anchors, objectives, and interventions are dormant beforehand.
- Branch nonrealization leaves a world latent and never fabricates retirement.

## Retirement

- Only an active world's own irreversible law can carry `retire-self`.
- Retirement stops later law execution and deactivates anchor/World-Will surfaces.
- It does not delete the world.
- The retained residue includes exact final fields, final digest, lineage, local negative sea, committed-memory ids, and retirement cause.
- Persistent continuation preserves retired phase and cannot silently reinitialize the world as latent or active.

## Membrane rule

An internal-law guard can bind only fields in its own world. The addition of a second active world exposed the previous generic binding surface as an unanchored information channel. `v0.5.6` rejects that shortcut. Cross-world content must wait for a typed anchor/sea transfer contract with source, target, ports, identity, causality, and replay provenance.

## Causal Bubble source

The additive `causal bubble` profile lowers authored exact worlds, fields, protected structure, sea coupling and weights, anchors, World-Will objectives, local predicates, effects, lifecycle transitions, inverses, and commits into `bubble-anchored-causal-program.v2`.

It does not reinterpret legacy `effect spawn` or observation `collapse` declarations. Legacy language profiles retain their original meanings.

The source profile is hardened by:

- one exact runtime validator for JSON and source-lowered programs
- no foreign-world guard syntax
- compiler rejection of reversible lifecycle laws and incomplete inverse contracts
- explicit opposite additive `law-inverse` effects for reversible field laws
- exact-kind guard checking and rational-only order comparisons
- ordinary run, inspection, record, and replay CLI paths

## What v0.5.6 does not claim

`v0.5.6` does not establish:

- runtime generation of new field schemas, laws, or open-ended populations
- an unbounded or fully dynamic multiverse
- global positive- or negative-sea transport
- typed cross-world anchor transfer
- full anchor identity or unique-world topology
- deliberation, agency, life, relationship, protagonist, or story
- general coinduction, continuous dynamics, or probability-cloud identity
- portable BIR, canonical binary storage, native execution, or Rust
- trans-Turing computation or a physical hypercomputation substrate
- Phase 2 observer work, recursive self-construction, or OB-001 whole-universe completion

The next 5.x core seam is typed cross-world anchor/sea transfer. Portable semantic representation and conformance/cost fixtures follow before Rust begins in 6.x.
